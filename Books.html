Favorite Books

Make It Stick

This is an awesome explanation of how best to learn new material. They talk a lot about what methods are proven to work. They even break it down to examples and support it with real life studies. If you ever felt lost on how to approach learning something difficult, this book would be a great read.

No Excuses

Brain Tracy the author, has been around for a while in the self-help/self improvement world. I have only read this one book from him but I plan on reading others later. Its a easy read or listen if you prefer audio books. What I like about this book is that his advice is timeless and your read other books in the field you can see parallels. This book is more of a doing book so I would say it's best read to read it in sections and the methods on improvements incorporated slowly.

Outliers

The author is half Jamaican as told through his a little story of his mother as a outlier towards the end of the book. Although this is a small mention and is over shadowed by the sheer novel* of this book it still lead me to have a even stronger infinity for this book. It totally changed my outlook on success and what it really is through the lense of statistical outlier. I highly recommend this book it's an awesome read.

The Millionaire Next Door

have you ever wondered what millionaire's really look like in American. What is their demographic? What set's them apart? The difference between being wealthy and earning a high income? If you ever wondered this book give you the answers and if not it's still a good read.
